  <section class="page" id="roadmap-module">
    <h2 class="module-title">Module 2: What You'll Learn & The Roadmap 🗺️</h2>

    <article>
      <p>In this course, we'll explore exciting fundamentals, one at a time. Think of each one as a new tool, skill,
        or superpower you can collect on your journey.</p>


      <div class="roadmap-container">
        <div class="topic-item">
          <a href="#variables-module" class="scroll-link">1. Variables 📦</a>
          <p>Variables are like labeled boxes for storing information — numbers, words, or even lists. In the
            exercises,
            you'll create your own variables, update them, and see how your program remembers each change. This helps
            you
            store and reuse data throughout your code, just like keeping your LEGO bricks organized for building.</p>
        </div>

        <div class="topic-item">
          <a href="#operations-module" class="scroll-link">2. Order of Operations 🧮</a>
          <p>Order of Operations ensures calculations happen in the correct sequence — parentheses first, then
            multiplication/division, and finally addition/subtraction, all following left-to-right rules. In the
            exercises, you'll evaluate expressions step by step, use brackets to control outcomes, and practice
            real-world
            examples like label rolls, bags per pallet, and averaging production. This builds a solid foundation for
            solving any calculation in code reliably.</p>
        </div>

        <div class="topic-item">
          <a href="#conditions-1-module" class="scroll-link">3. Conditions Part 1: Basic Comparisons ❓</a>
          <p>Conditions let your program make decisions by asking "if" questions. Learn about basic comparison
            operators
            like greater than (&gt;) and less than (&lt;). Your code checks conditions and decides what to do — your
            first
            step in teaching programs to think and make choices!</p>
        </div>

        <div class="topic-item">
          <a href="#conditions-2-module" class="scroll-link">4. Conditions Part 2: Equality Checks 🔄</a>
          <p>Continue building your conditional skills! Part 2 introduces more comparison operators — equal to (===)
            and
            not equal to (!==). Learn how to check if values match exactly or are different, giving you more precise
            ways
            to make decisions in your code.</p>
        </div>

        <div class="topic-item">
          <a href="#conditions-3-module" class="scroll-link">5. Conditions Part 3: <= and>= 🔀</a>
          <p>Learn about inclusive comparisons with less than or equal to (&lt;=) and greater than or equal to
            (&gt;=).
            These operators help you set boundaries and limits in your code, perfect for checking if something is
            within a
            safe range or meets minimum requirements.</p>
        </div>

        <div class="topic-item">
          <a href="#conditions-4-module" class="scroll-link">6. Conditions Part 4: If/Else + Multiple Conditions
            🏭</a>
          <p>Ready to handle both outcomes? Learn if/else statements and else if chains. Your code can choose between
            multiple paths, making smarter, more complete decisions. Practice with classroom scenarios like game
            access
            and homework checks.</p>
        </div>

        <div class="topic-item">
          <a href="#conditions-5-module" class="scroll-link">7. Conditions Part 5: Else If + Multiple Outcomes 🎓</a>
          <p>Level up to advanced decision-making! Use else if to handle multiple possible outcomes. Perfect for
            grading
            systems, reading levels, and other scenarios where you need more than just two choices.</p>
        </div>

        <div class="topic-item">
          <a href="#logical-1-module" class="scroll-link">8. Logical Operators Part 1: AND (&&) 🔒</a>
          <p>Learn the AND operator that requires BOTH conditions to be true. Just like needing both a key AND a code
            to
            open a safe. Practice with popcorn factory safety checks where multiple conditions must be met.</p>
        </div>

        <div class="topic-item">
          <a href="#logical-2-module" class="scroll-link">9. Logical Operators Part 2: OR (||) 🚨</a>
          <p>Master the OR operator where EITHER condition can be true. Like emergency stop buttons — press either one
            to
            stop the machine! Learn how to give your code flexible decision-making options.</p>
        </div>

        <!-- LOOPS MODULES - READY -->
        <div class="topic-item">
          <a href="#loops-part-1-module" class="scroll-link">10. Loops Part 1: Basic Counting 🔁</a>
          <p>Learn how to make your code repeat automatically! Use for loops to count, track supplies, and avoid
            writing
            the same code over and over. Practice with addition and multiplication inside loops. Perfect for counting
            bags, stickers, and pencils!</p>
        </div>

        <div class="topic-item">
          <a href="#loops-part-2-module" class="scroll-link">11. Loops Part 2: Subtraction & Division ➖➗</a>
          <p>Take loops further with subtraction and division! Track items decreasing over time and share things
            equally
            among groups. Practice with erasers, cookies, pages, and teams. Learn to calculate what's left and how to
            share fairly!</p>
        </div>

        <!-- COMING SOON MODULES -->
        <div class="topic-item">
          <a href="#loops-part-3-module" class="scroll-link">12. Loops Part 3: Loops + Conditions 🔄❓ (Coming
            Soon)</a>
          <p>Combine loops with if statements to make smarter repetitions! Learn to skip items, stop early, or only
            count
            certain things. Add the 🟢 GREEN and 🟡 YELLOW paths to your loop toolkit.</p>
        </div>

        <div class="topic-item">
          <a href="#pirate-1-module" class="scroll-link">13. Pirate Adventure Part 1: Ship Challenges 🏴‍☠️ (Coming
            Soon)</a>
          <p>Join Captain Condition on a pirate adventure! Review everything you've learned about conditions in
            exciting
            pirate scenarios. Solve puzzles to board the ship, read treasure maps, and fire cannons!</p>
        </div>

        <div class="topic-item">
          <a href="#pirate-2-module" class="scroll-link">14. Pirate Adventure Part 2: Treasure Hunt 💎 (Coming
            Soon)</a>
          <p>The adventure continues on Treasure Island! Combine all your skills to find buried treasure. Solve
            complex puzzles, navigate jungle paths, and finally unlock the treasure chest!</p>
        </div>

        <div class="topic-item">
          <a href="#functions-module" class="scroll-link">15. Functions 🧩 (Coming Soon)</a>
          <p>Think of a function like a lunchbox recipe: once you write down how to make your favorite sandwich, you
            don't
            have to rewrite the steps every time you're hungry. In code, a function saves a set of instructions under
            a
            name, so you can run them again and again with just one word.</p>
        </div>

        <div class="topic-item">
          <a href="#arrays-module" class="scroll-link">16. Arrays 📋 (Coming Soon)</a>
          <p>An array is like a numbered list that keeps things in order — your top 5 songs, letters in your name, or
            friends' birthdays. You can ask for item #3, add new items to the end, or count how many you have.</p>
        </div>

        <div class="topic-item">
          <a href="#objects-module" class="scroll-link">17. Objects 📦 (Coming Soon)</a>
          <p>An object is like a profile card that holds different kinds of information about one thing. A robot might
            have a name, color, battery level, and mood — each with its own label so you can find exactly what you
            need.
          </p>
        </div>

        <div class="topic-item">
          <a href="#events-module" class="scroll-link">18. Events ⚡ (Coming Soon)</a>
          <p>Events are how your program listens and responds to what you do — clicking buttons, pressing keys, or
            moving
            your mouse. When you play a game or use an app, events make it react instantly to your actions.</p>
        </div>

        <p>
          "We're now stepping into a fun adventure full of experiments, discoveries, and 'aha!' moments. After
          learning
          each
          fundamental — through hands-on exercises and playful challenges — we'll start mixing them together to see
          how
          they
          connect. That's when things get really exciting! You'll begin to see how these pieces fit and work as one,
          helping
          you build, create, and explore even more possibilities". Let's dive in and start with our first fundamental:
          Variables.
        </p>
    </article>
  </section>

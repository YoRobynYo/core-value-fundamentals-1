Module 1: Introduction to Fundamentals

Imagine each fundamental as a single LEGO brick. At first, it might not look very exciting — just a small, colorful block sitting by itself. But what happens when you add another? And another? Suddenly, you can make a wall… then a little house… then maybe a castle with towers and a drawbridge. Keep going, and you could build rockets, robots, or even entire worlds!

That’s what learning the fundamentals is like. Each new idea might seem tiny at first, but when you connect them, they start to form something amazing. These are the basic ideas that almost everything in technology is built on — the patterns, logic, and structure that help us tell computers what to do. They’re the building blocks that turn imagination into creation.

Learning these first is like laying a strong foundation before building a treehouse or learning your ABCs before writing a story. Once you understand these pieces, you’ll know not just what to do, but why it works — and that’s where the real magic happens.

You’ll soon discover that things like variables, loops, and functions are like special tools in your thinking toolbox. They help you give clear instructions, notice patterns, remember important information, and make smart decisions — just like you do when solving puzzles or planning an adventure.

Module 2: What You'll Learn & The Roadmap 🗺️

In this course, we'll explore exciting fundamentals, one at a time. Think of each one as a new tool, skill, or superpower you can collect on your journey.

1. Variables 📦

Variables are like labeled boxes for storing information — numbers, words, or even lists. In the exercises, you'll create your own variables, update them, and see how your program remembers each change. This helps you store and reuse data throughout your code, just like keeping your LEGO bricks organized for building.

2. Order of Operations 🧮

Order of Operations ensures calculations happen in the correct sequence — parentheses first, then multiplication/division, and finally addition/subtraction, all following left-to-right rules. In the exercises, you'll evaluate expressions step by step, use brackets to control outcomes, and practice real-world examples like label rolls, bags per pallet, and averaging production. This builds a solid foundation for solving any calculation in code reliably.

3. Conditions Part 1: Basic Comparisons ❓

Conditions let your program make decisions by asking "if" questions. Learn about basic comparison operators like greater than (>) and less than (<). Your code checks conditions and decides what to do — your first step in teaching programs to think and make choices!

4. Conditions Part 2: Equality Checks 🔄

Continue building your conditional skills! Part 2 introduces more comparison operators — equal to (===) and not equal to (!==). Learn how to check if values match exactly or are different, giving you more precise ways to make decisions in your code.

5. Conditions Part 3: <= and>= 🔀

Learn about inclusive comparisons with less than or equal to (<=) and greater than or equal to (>=). These operators help you set boundaries and limits in your code, perfect for checking if something is within a safe range or meets minimum requirements.

6. Conditions Part 4: If/Else + Multiple Conditions 🏭

Ready to handle both outcomes? Learn if/else statements and else if chains. Your code can choose between multiple paths, making smarter, more complete decisions. Practice with classroom scenarios like game access and homework checks.

7. Conditions Part 5: Else If + Multiple Outcomes 🎓

Level up to advanced decision-making! Use else if to handle multiple possible outcomes. Perfect for grading systems, reading levels, and other scenarios where you need more than just two choices.

8. Logical Operators Part 1: AND (&&) 🔒

Learn the AND operator that requires BOTH conditions to be true. Just like needing both a key AND a code to open a safe. Practice with popcorn factory safety checks where multiple conditions must be met.

9. Logical Operators Part 2: OR (||) 🚨

Master the OR operator where EITHER condition can be true. Like emergency stop buttons — press either one to stop the machine! Learn how to give your code flexible decision-making options.

10. Loops Part 1: Basic Counting 🔁

Learn how to make your code repeat automatically! Use for loops to count, track supplies, and avoid writing the same code over and over. Practice with addition and multiplication inside loops. Perfect for counting bags, stickers, and pencils!

11. Loops Part 2: Subtraction & Division ➖➗

Take loops further with subtraction and division! Track items decreasing over time and share things equally among groups. Practice with erasers, cookies, pages, and teams. Learn to calculate what's left and how to share fairly!

12. Loops Part 3: Loops + Conditions 🔄❓ (Coming Soon)

Combine loops with if statements to make smarter repetitions! Learn to skip items, stop early, or only count certain things. Add the 🟢 GREEN and 🟡 YELLOW paths to your loop toolkit.

13. Pirate Adventure Part 1: Ship Challenges 🏴‍☠️ (Coming Soon)

Join Captain Condition on a pirate adventure! Review everything you've learned about conditions in exciting pirate scenarios. Solve puzzles to board the ship, read treasure maps, and fire cannons!

14. Pirate Adventure Part 2: Treasure Hunt 💎 (Coming Soon)

The adventure continues on Treasure Island! Combine all your skills to find buried treasure. Solve complex puzzles, navigate jungle paths, and finally unlock the treasure chest!

15. Functions 🧩 (Coming Soon)

Think of a function like a lunchbox recipe: once you write down how to make your favorite sandwich, you don't have to rewrite the steps every time you're hungry. In code, a function saves a set of instructions under a name, so you can run them again and again with just one word.

16. Arrays 📋 (Coming Soon)

An array is like a numbered list that keeps things in order — your top 5 songs, letters in your name, or friends' birthdays. You can ask for item #3, add new items to the end, or count how many you have.

17. Objects 📦 (Coming Soon)

An object is like a profile card that holds different kinds of information about one thing. A robot might have a name, color, battery level, and mood — each with its own label so you can find exactly what you need.

18. Events ⚡ (Coming Soon)

Events are how your program listens and responds to what you do — clicking buttons, pressing keys, or moving your mouse. When you play a game or use an app, events make it react instantly to your actions.
